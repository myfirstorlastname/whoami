ğŸ” Overview

Active Directory (AD) is Microsoft's centralized identity and access management system used in most enterprise environments. It controls authentication, user roles, group policies, and access to resources across the domain.

Defensive Perspective: Securing AD involves implementing strong Group Policies (GPOs), enforcing tiered administration, auditing critical changes, and monitoring for anomalous behaviors. Modern environments also integrate multifactor authentication (MFA) and privileged access management (PAM).

Red Team Perspective: AD is often the prime target for attackers. Typical goals include credential harvesting, lateral movement, and domain privilege escalation. Tools like BloodHound and SharpHound assist in AD enumeration, identifying misconfigurations and privilege paths that can be abused to reach Domain Adminâ€”a full compromise of identity infrastructure.

Understanding both attack and defense in AD is essential for securing enterprise-scale environments.
