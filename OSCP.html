Roadmap to OSCP

From Zero to OSCP : What to do without prior experience ?
  • To be honnest : I was not starting from Zero, I had some basic knowledge from engineering school and a first blue team experience as an intern.
  • But those experiences gave me no deep technical/theoretical understanding of the red team world.
  • I considered myself a true beginner.

The first step : CTFs

  • My first real step into hacking was to give myself time : time to learn.
  • I first learned how much of a beginner I was : everything was new to me, I had some basic knowledge on a few things, but that's it.
  • How did I learn this lesson ? I challenged the CTFs on RootMe in no specific order.
  • After some time, I sharpened my skills to find where the flags are hidden, but that's no hacking.
  • After one year I had some good rank on the RootMe website, so I decided to move on with something new.
  • During that year I realised that web was easier than systems and such, so I decided to specialize myself in web.

The second step : Specialization

  • This step was imposed to me as I couldn't solve any more CTFs on RootMe (too hard).
  • I searched on the internet a new CTF-like website specialized in web hacking for beginners, I found : webhacking.kr
  • There, I learned what is OWASP top 10. In two months I finished almost all the CTFs on this site, it was a real fast scale-up of my skills.
  • Once done with webhacking.kr, and following jobs interviewers' advice (as I was refused to many pentest jobs), I started my real work on OWASP : PortSwigger
  • PortSwigger was developped by the OWASP Foundation, they also developped THE web tool : Burp Suite.
  • I spent months on these labs : XSS, SQLi, CSRF, HTTP Request Smuggling, to finally get an overall understanding of the background mechanisms on the web.
  • I got a good understanding of Client-side browsers, Server-side requests, Front-end/Back-end Servers, Web Application Firewalls, Databases.

The third step : Real-world

  • This step was imposed to me as I still had no income, I chose to work as a freelance bug bounty hunter on HackerOne.
  • I found some small vulnerabilites that I reported with exploits, but these gave me no income as they were duplicates : $0.
  • The only way to get paid is to be on an old program already 99.9% secure, and still find the small vulnerability hidden. But honestly even if you find one, the small bounty isn't worse the time.
  • However I learnt some valuable skills : automation, R&D, how to use Burp Suite more efficiently...

The fourth step : Blue team

  • As I still had no income two years after my engineer graduation (and persistently applying for every red team opportunity), I decided to go back to blue team.
  • To have a chance in a SOC environment (after two years), I had to rebuild my blue team skillset from scratch without a proper env.
  • That's how I discovered TryHackMe labs. Their labs are great and really well documented. Lots of forums on THM labs.
  • I passed the SOC N1 cert delivered by THM, and began the N2 course.
  • With this SOC knowledge, I decided to apply to every blue team opportunity. (and still applied to red team)

The fifth step : OSCP ?

  • I already heard of the OSCP and other certs such as Burp Suite cert, but never asked myself : Why would I not attempt it ?
  • Often some interviewer gave me the advice to get the OSCP. During these times, I had really good technical knowledge on web, and they felt it (but I suppose there's always someone better for the job...)
  • Seeing my limitations : I had the skills, but still couldn't get a job ; I decided to invest time specifically for preparing the OSCP.
  • What is OSCP ?
  • It's a certification delivered by Offsec that says : This guy has basic professionnal red team skills.
  • It's focused on Active Directory (AD) and with some web hacking parts.
  • I was specialized in web hacking, but had no skills on AD, so I turned to TryHackme.
  • TryHackMe has some really good red team courses : I began immediatly with the AD labs.
  • I started with Attacktive Directory, then Breaching AD, Enumerating AD, ...
  • That's where I'm at today.

The last step : get the OSCP and a job !
