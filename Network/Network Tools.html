Network tools are critical for security professionals, sysadmins, and blue teamers. They help inspect traffic, identify anomalies, analyze packets, and trace malicious activities across the network.

This page summarizes my experience and use cases with some of the most common and powerful tools for network inspection and investigation.

üî¨ Packet Analysis Tools

  • Wireshark: GUI-based packet analyzer. Perfect for dissecting protocols visually and in-depth.
  • tshark: Terminal version of Wireshark. Ideal for headless systems or scripting.
  • tcpdump: Lightweight command-line sniffer. Great for quick captures or scripting with grep, awk, etc.

üß™ Diagnostic & Traffic Tools

  • Netcat: The ‚ÄúSwiss Army knife‚Äù of networking. Can create reverse shells, transfer files, or test ports.
  • nmap: For scanning and enumerating hosts, open ports, services, and OS versions.
  • ss / netstat: For viewing open sockets and established connections.
  • iftop / iptraf-ng: Real-time bandwidth monitoring tools.

üîç SOC/Blue Team Usage

  • Traffic filtering to detect suspicious payloads.
  • PCAP review during incident response or triage.
  • Correlating IP, ports, protocols, and timestamps from captured sessions.
  • Validating alerts from IDS/IPS systems (like Suricata or Snort).
