Client Communication : Pentest Consultant

Good hacks are invisible. Good reports make them unforgettable.

Goal: Be Understood by Non-Technical Stakeholders

As a pentest consultant, you often need to present technical findings to business executives or clients who may not have a technical background. Your mission is to:

  • Avoid technical jargon
  • Communicate business risks clearly
  • Offer actionable and understandable recommendations

Translate Technical Findings into Business Impact

Instead of saying:

“Your Tomcat server is vulnerable to CVE-2021-44228 on port 8080.”

Say:

“We found a flaw on a public server that could allow attackers to take full control. This vulnerability has been used in real-world attacks by cybercriminals.”

Instead of “LFI (Local File Inclusion)”:

“An attacker could read sensitive files on your server, such as passwords or internal configuration, without needing to log in.”

Build a Clear, Impactful Message

  • Finding: “We simulated an external attacker and accessed...”
  • Risk: “This gives access to sensitive client data.”
  • Likelihood: “This is a well-known vulnerability actively exploited.”
  • Recommendation: “We strongly recommend applying this fix within 7 days.”

Clear Wording Examples

  • Technical: “Reflected XSS on GET parameter ‘search’.”
  • Business-oriented: “An attacker could trick users into running malicious code in their browser via your website.”
  • Technical: “No rate limiting on login form.”
  • Business-oriented: “An attacker could try thousands of passwords in minutes without being blocked.”

General Tips for Executive Briefings

  • Focus on what could happen, not what you “hacked”
  • Use analogies: “This is like leaving a backdoor unlocked”
  • Provide prioritized, realistic actions
  • Show that you understand their business context

Sample Verbal Summary

“In summary, we identified 3 critical issues: public access to a sensitive server, weak authentication controls, and lack of logging. These haven’t been exploited yet, but they expose your business to real threats. We suggest addressing the top 2 risks immediately.”
Clear, concise, and contextual : your ability to communicate findings determines their real-world value.
