🧠 About

This page covers the Phishing Analysis module from the SOC Level 1 learning path on TryHackMe.
It focuses on the identification, investigation, and prevention of phishing emails — one of the most common attack vectors in enterprise environments. The module trains both technical analysis (headers, payloads, URLs) and contextual investigation.

🛰️ Philosophy

"A single click can breach a network. A trained eye can stop it."
  • Phishing isn't just about spam detection — it's about context, intent, and deception.
  • As an analyst, knowing how to dissect a suspicious email is key to:
    • Identifying threats early
    • Improving user awareness
    • Protecting broader infrastructure

📘 Topics and Labs Covered

From fake invoices to malware loaders

📧 Phishing Analysis Fundamentals

  • Types of phishing: credential harvesting, malicious attachments, spear phishing, whaling
  • How attackers craft convincing lures and evade detection
  • Email header structure and spoofing techniques

🔬 Phishing Emails in Action

  • Hands-on analysis of real-world phishing samples
  • Extracting indicators from headers and content
  • Identifying obfuscation techniques and social engineering clues

🛠️ Phishing Analysis Tools

  • Using:
    • email-header-analyzer and phish.tool
    • VirusTotal and URLScan for link checking
    • OLETools for attachment analysis (Office macros)
    • CyberChef for decoding or deobfuscating payloads

🛡️ Phishing Prevention

  • SPF, DKIM, DMARC — how email authentication works
  • Security awareness tips to reduce user-level risk
  • Technical countermeasures (sandboxing, filtering, quarantining)

🎯 Phishing Challenges

  • The Greenholt Phish – Full investigation of a phishing case: headers, links, attachment, and campaign context
  • Snapped Phish-ing Line – A challenge in reconstructing attacker intent from partial evidence

  • I can identify spoofing, encoding tricks, and track threat actors via recurring patterns

  • I see phishing not just as an attack, but as an opportunity to educate and defend
