🛰️ Philosophy

"Frameworks are mental maps. The better the map, the faster the response."
  • Cybersecurity isn't just technical—it’s strategic. Frameworks give structure to:
    • How we detect and respond to threats
    • How attackers operate and evolve
    • How teams share a common vocabulary across disciplines
  • Learning to think in models like MITRE or the Kill Chain is key to building good habits as an analyst.

📘 Key Concepts Explored

A mental toolbox for organizing and communicating defense

🧱 Junior Security Analyst Introduction

  • Understanding the role of a SOC L1 and how frameworks guide triage and escalation.

🗿 Pyramid of Pain

  • Different types of indicators have different values. This framework shows why detecting TTPs (tactics, techniques, and procedures) is more impactful than blocking IPs.

🔗 Cyber Kill Chain

  • The classic Lockheed Martin model. Visualizes the attacker lifecycle from reconnaissance to action on objectives.
  • Useful to anticipate the next step an attacker might take if not stopped early.

🔗 Unified Kill Chain

  • A more modern and complete version that combines both pre- and post-exploitation phases.
  • It integrates concepts from MITRE ATT&CK and the Kill Chain into a single, extended timeline.

💠 Diamond Model

  • A framework for understanding threat intelligence with four key components: adversary, infrastructure, capability, and victim.
  • Valuable for writing threat reports and correlating indicators of compromise.

🧬 MITRE ATT&CK

  • The most widely adopted framework for mapping adversary behavior.
  • Each technique is documented with detection ideas, mitigations, and references.

📈 Threat Intelligence Lifecycle (Summit)

  • Focuses on how CTI is produced—from planning to dissemination.
  • Highlights the importance of context and human interpretation.

🚪 Eviction Process

  • Framework used to guide the safe and strategic removal of threat actors from compromised environments.
  • Stresses timing, containment, and coordination with incident response teams.
